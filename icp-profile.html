✅ Strong Fit Signals
  • ✓ Independent RIA — no broker-dealer product list to navigate
  • ✓ Client base is accredited investors ($1M+ liquid)
  • ✓ Already recommends private placements or alternative investments
  • ✓ Advisors talk about tax efficiency and depreciation with clients
  • ✓ Firm principal makes investment decisions (not committee-driven)
  • ✓ Clients ask about real estate but advisor doesn't want to manage it
  • ✓ Advisor has relationship depth — knows client balance sheets
🚫 Disqualifying Signals
  • ✗ Wirehouse or captive broker (Merrill, UBS, Morgan Stanley)
  • ✗ Primarily retail / mass affluent under $500K investable
  • ✗ Firm has an approved product list that excludes Reg D offerings
  • ✗ Investment committee controls all alternative allocation decisions
  • ✗ Advisor is anti-real estate or has had bad REIT experience
  • ✗ Purely transactional — no ongoing client relationship model
⚡ High-Conviction Triggers
  • ★ Client recently sold a business or liquidity event — needs deployment
  • ★ Client has 1031 exchange need (Tritower can sometimes be a solution)
  • ★ Advisor is consolidating alternatives into 3–5 trusted partners
  • ★ New advisor at a firm already familiar with Tritower
  • ★ Advisor is at a firm that recently went independent from a wirehouse
🤝 Relationship Entry Points
  • → Shared North Shore community (kids' schools, charity events, clubs)
  • → CFA / CFP study group or local chapter connections
  • → FINRA / SEC compliance conversation (Patrick is Series 79-qualified)
  • → Boston-area CRE market conversation — Patrick knows the inventory
  • → Mutual referral with a CPA or estate attorney

The Ideal Partner — "Sarah at Northshore Wealth"

A composite persona representing Tritower's best-fit referral relationship

Sarah has been an independent RIA for eleven years, left a wirehouse after realizing she couldn't recommend what she actually believed in. Her book is 85 families — most of them business owners, a few physicians, one or two inherited wealth. Average household has $2.5M with her, maybe $5M total. She's fee-only, she's proud of it, and her clients trust her completely.

She's been asked about real estate for years. She's seen what happens when clients DIY it — the midnight calls about tenants, the deferred cap gains that ballooned into a problem. She doesn't want to be a real estate advisor. But she does want a trusted partner who can handle the real estate conversation so she doesn't lose the allocation to someone else.

She's not going to recommend anyone she doesn't know. The first meeting with Patrick is about trust, not product. She wants to understand how Tritower thinks about downside, how they've handled difficult tenants, what happens in a default scenario. If those answers are good — and she knows her clients are good fits — she'll send business, and she'll send it repeatedly.

She lives in Marblehead. Patrick probably knows someone who knows her. That's the real ICP — not a firmographic profile, but a human being who is already looking for exactly what Tritower offers, and just needs a trusted introduction.

🗣️ Conversation Starters Claude Generates for Each Lead

OPENER
"We've been doing a lot of work with advisors up here on the North Shore — it feels like there's a real appetite for real asset exposure that isn't the public REIT market. Is that something your clients are asking about?"
TRUST
"The way we think about it: advisors are the fiduciary, we're the real estate operator. You never have to explain a balance sheet or a tenant — that's on us. Your job is to decide if the structure fits your client's allocation."
OBJECTION
"I understand the illiquidity concern — it's real. We structure everything with a defined hold period so clients can plan around it. And compared to a direct property, there's no management overhead, no 3am calls."
CLOSE
"Would it be worth a 30-minute call where I walk you through the last two deals — the property, the tenant, the return structure — so you can see exactly what your clients would be investing in?"
